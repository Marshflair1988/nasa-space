Live Space Data

Who is in space right now and where the International Space Station is in its orbit around Earth.

Humans have been living and working in space continuously since the arrival of the first ISS crew in 2000. Today, astronauts and cosmonauts from NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, and CSA serve on the International Space Station, and China’s Tiangong station also hosts crews. The sections below show the current crew and the ISS position so you can see who is in space and where the station is over Earth.

Crew size changes when new missions launch and when others return to Earth. NASA astronauts often fly to the ISS on SpaceX Crew Dragon spacecraft and spend about six months in orbit conducting research and maintaining the station.

ISS Location

The International Space Station orbits Earth at about 400 km altitude, completing one orbit roughly every 90 minutes. The latitude and longitude below show the point on Earth directly beneath the station at the time shown. Because the ISS travels at about 28,000 km/h, its position changes constantly; the coordinates help you visualise where the station is over the planet (e.g. over an ocean, continent, or city).
